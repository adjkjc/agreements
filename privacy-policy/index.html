Hypothesis Privacy Policy

Hypothesis cares about your privacy and does not share your personal data with anyone.

User Information

We keep a fairly limited amount of information in the user table: username, email and last login date. We have optional fields for a display name, location and a description on your profile.

Access to this information is restricted to system administrators. The Hypothesis staff has limited access to user data for support purposes (email, username, active status, number of annotations, group membership)

Cookies

Hypothesis may place a number of cookie files on your web browser. By using the service you consent to the use of cookies.

Cookies are small pieces of text sent to your web browser by a website you visit. A cookie file is stored in your web browser and allows our service to recognize you and make your next visit easier and more useful.

If you'd like to delete cookies or instruct your web browser to delete or refuse cookies, please visit the help pages of your web browser. Please note, however, that if you delete cookies or refuse to accept them, you might not be able to use all of the features we offer, you may not be able to store your preferences, and some of our pages might not display properly.

Handling user data

User passwords are salted and hashed using the industry standard “bcrypt” algorithm. No Hypothesis staff member has access to user passwords.

We retain user IP address information and request information for a strictly limited period (2 weeks) for diagnostic purposes only. We also collect some information about your activities in our application from the client, for logging purposes and statistics.

On third parties:

  • We use Sentry to track errors that occur within our software. User IP address information and usernames may be sent to Sentry for diagnostic purposes. Sentry’s privacy policy: https://getsentry.com/privacy/.
  • We use Cloudflare logs, which use IP and url information, and it's kept no longer than 4 hours. Cloudlfare privacy policy: https://www.cloudflare.com/security-policy/
  • We use Google Analytics for our extension and website. Google analytics privacy policy: https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/6004245?hl=en
  • We use AWS hosting, located in the “us-west-1” region in N. California. AWS privacy policy: https://aws.amazon.com/privacy/
  • We use Mailchimp for user communications, including registration, email replies and our newsletter. Mailchimp Privacy Policy: https://mailchimp.com/legal/privacy/

Hypothesis doesn’t share user information with advertisers, affiliates or partners. We do not monetize our customer traffic in any way.

Access to annotation data

There are a few ways in which annotations are available to other users:

  • Annotations made in the public channel are visible to every Hypothesis user
  • Annotations made in groups are only visible to group members
  • Annotations published for “Only Me” are only visible to the person who made the annotation

The same applies to our API users: authenticated users can only retrieve annotations for which they have the right permissions. Unauthenticated users can only retrieve public annotations.

With respect to Hypothesis staff, a small number of engineering staff have database access that lets them see any annotation or profile data in your account.

Chrome extension permissions

The Hypothesis extension for Chrome will ask users to grant certain permissions at the time of installation. The extension needs them in order to work with the user’s browser and the websites the user visits. More detailed information about how we use these permissions on this blog post: https://hypothes.is/blog/hypothes-is-chrome-extension-permissions/

Contact with users

We send account registration, activation and password reset notifications via email. We also send event notifications such as about replies to annotations via email, with the option to opt-out. Approximately every quarter we send out a newsletter, “The Margin”, with the option to opt-out. In the future we may also send information about important service updates or substantial changes in Privacy Policy or our Terms of Service.
